Introduction

Information We Collect

Information You Provide

When you use Cascade, we collect information that you voluntarily provide:

  • Account Information: When you sign in with Apple, we receive your user identifier and optionally your email (if you choose to share it)
  • Habit Data: Habit names, descriptions, completion dates, and streak information
  • User Preferences: Theme selections, language preferences, and display settings

Information Automatically Collected

  • Device Information: iOS version and device type (for app compatibility only)
  • App Usage: Habit completion patterns and feature usage (stored locally and in your account only)

How We Use Your Information

We use your information solely to provide and improve the Cascade app experience:

  • Core Functionality: To save, sync, and display your habits across your devices
  • Progress Tracking: To calculate streaks, statistics, and achievements
  • Personalization: To apply your theme and language preferences
  • Account Management: To enable Sign in with Apple and sync data across devices
  • Customer Support: To respond to your support requests

Data Storage and Security

Where Your Data is Stored

  • Cloud Storage: Your habit data is stored in Google Firebase Firestore with encryption at rest
  • Local Storage: Preferences and cached data are stored locally on your device
  • Authentication: Managed securely through Firebase Authentication

Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures:

  • Encrypted data transmission (HTTPS/TLS)
  • Secure authentication via Sign in with Apple
  • Firebase security rules to ensure users can only access their own data
  • No passwords stored (authentication handled by Apple)

Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not:

  • Sell, rent, or trade your personal information
  • Share your data with third parties for marketing purposes
  • Use your data for advertising
  • Track you across other apps or websites

We only share data when:

  • Required by law or legal process
  • Necessary to protect our rights or comply with legal obligations
  • You explicitly request us to (e.g., customer support)

Your Rights and Choices

Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access: View all data associated with your account
  • Correction: Edit or update your habits and preferences
  • Deletion: Delete individual habits or your entire account
  • Portability: Export your data (feature coming soon)
  • Opt-out: Use the app without an account (local storage only)

How to Exercise Your Rights

  • Delete Habits: Swipe to delete or archive any habit
  • Delete Account: Sign out and request account deletion via email
  • Access Data: All your data is visible within the app
  • Contact Us: Email us for any privacy-related requests

Children's Privacy

Cascade is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe we have inadvertently collected such information, please contact us immediately.

Third-Party Services

Cascade uses the following third-party services:

Firebase (Google)

  • Purpose: Authentication and data storage
  • Data: User ID, email (optional), habit data
  • Privacy Policy: firebase.google.com/support/privacy

Apple Sign In

  • Purpose: User authentication
  • Data: User identifier, email (optional)
  • Privacy Policy: apple.com/legal/privacy

StoreKit (Apple)

  • Purpose: In-app purchases for Cascade Pro
  • Data: Purchase history (managed by Apple)
  • Privacy Policy: apple.com/legal/privacy

Data Retention

  • Active Accounts: We retain your data as long as your account is active
  • Archived Habits: Retained until you choose to permanently delete them
  • Deleted Accounts: Data is permanently deleted within 30 days of account deletion request
  • Local Data: Remains on device until app is uninstalled

International Data Transfers

Your data may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your own, specifically where Firebase servers are located. We ensure appropriate safeguards are in place for such transfers.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. We will notify you of any material changes by:

  • Updating the "Last Updated" date
  • Showing an in-app notification for significant changes
  • Obtaining your consent where required by law

California Privacy Rights

California residents have additional rights under CCPA:

  • Right to know what personal information is collected
  • Right to know if personal information is sold or disclosed (we don't sell data)
  • Right to opt-out of sale (not applicable - we don't sell data)
  • Right to non-discrimination for exercising privacy rights

European Privacy Rights (GDPR)

If you're in the European Economic Area, you have rights under GDPR:

  • Legal basis for processing: Contract and legitimate interests
  • Right to object to processing
  • Right to lodge a complaint with supervisory authorities
  • Right to withdraw consent
